Call for Workshops

The WiOpt 2024 will be held in Korea University, Seoul, Republic of Korea, Oct. 2024. In addition to the main WiOpt symposium technical program, it will include a set of In-Person Workshops held on Oct. 21, 2024.

The goals of the workshops are to explore emerging research topics, and to provide a forum for authors to present early research results on these topics. We would like to have a limited number of workshops, with a high-quality program dominated by contributed papers. Contributions to the workshops would be for presenting novel ideas in a less formal setting, possibly more focused on a particular topic than the regular symposium sessions do. While we expect that workshop papers would be less mature than a typical WiOpt symposium paper, they should present particularly novel ideas and approaches, which have the potential to make their way to full symposium papers in future editions. Therefore, we expect that workshops will run a rigorous review process, to make sure programs will be of high quality. We will interact with the chairs of the selected workshops after the review phase, to discuss with them about a suitable acceptance rate and format of the workshop, based on the set of submitted and accepted papers. We seek proposals from individuals and teams interested in organizing strong workshops. Ideally, each workshop will be a full day, with 4-5 sessions of 1.5 hours each, or half day. The dates for paper submission, notification, camera-ready version, and session times will be coordinated across the selected workshops. Papers submitted to the individual workshops should be due on June 15, with the paper acceptance notification due on August 6, and camera-ready version due on August 20. All workshop papers will be submitted for inclusion to IEEE Xplore, IFIP database as well as various Abstracting and Indexing databases. (If it is preferred to not include in the proceedings, please let us know.)

The workshop proposal, in PDF, should provide the following information, in the following format:

HEADING

*1. Title of the workshop

*2. Names and affiliations of the organization team

*3. Short bio of workshop organizers (no more than 150 words)

INTRODUCTION

*4. Scope and topics of the workshop

RATIONALE

*5. Rationale

**a. Why the workshop is related to WiOpt main symposium

**b. Why the topic is timely and important

**c. Why the workshop may attract a significant number of submissions of good quality and a large number of attendees, in addition to the authors

WORKSHOP DETAILS

*6. Tentative committee lists (organizers, steering committee if any, etc.)

*7. A draft call for papers

*8. Workshop tentative internal and external schedule (expect adjustments by the workshops chairs to align all workshop schedules)

**a. Submission deadline

**b. Review deadline

**c. Acceptance deadline

**d. Camera ready

*9. Workshop format planned (keynote, papers, demos, panels, etc.). We encourage the workshop organizers to consider inviting one keynote speaker.

*10. Whether the workshop is planned to be a full-day or half-day event

WORKSHOP HISTORY

*11. If the workshop has past editions, the history of the workshop


SUBMISSION INSTRUCTIONS

Please send a whole copy of your proposal in PDF format to: wiopt2024workshop@gmail.com with subject “Submission of WiOpt 2024 workshop proposal: XXX”, where XXX should be replaced by the short name of your workshop. The total length of the proposal cannot exceed 6 pages.
